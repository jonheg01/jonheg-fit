What the science actually says
E
Whole eggs -- eat as many as you want
Choline, leucine, fat-soluble vitamins A/D/E/K2, complete amino acids. The yolk is where all the nutrition lives. Dietary cholesterol limits dropped from USDA guidelines in 2015.
6-7g P/egg5g F/egg~80 cal
B
80/20 ground beef -- your best muscle-building food
Creatine, carnitine, zinc, iron, B12, complete protein. Do NOT buy lean beef. Saturated fat from ruminants is associated with higher testosterone in men.
22g P/4oz17g F/4oz~240 cal
M
Whole milk (non-homogenized) -- excellent choice
Non-homogenized preserves fat globule structure. Contains CLA. Casein protein digests slowly -- perfect before bed.
8g P/cup8g F/cup12g C/cup~150 cal
F
Butter and tallow -- replace all seed oils now
Cook in butter or beef tallow only. Seed oils oxidize at cooking temperatures generating inflammatory byproducts. Single most impactful food swap you can make.
R
White rice -- better than you have been told
Low in anti-nutrients compared to whole grains. Replenishes muscle glycogen post-workout.
45g C/cup cooked4g P~200 cal
L
Beef liver -- once per week
Most nutrient-dense food on earth. Blend 4 oz raw liver into raw ground beef at 20/80 ratio before cooking -- you will not taste it.

What mainstream American nutrition gets wrong
Mainstream claim
Saturated fat causes heart disease
The original Seven Countries Study cherry-picked data. Multiple independent meta-analyses found no significant association. The relevant markers are LDL particle size, triglycerides, and HDL -- all of which improve on your food plan.
Mainstream claim
Dietary cholesterol raises blood cholesterol
For most people, dietary cholesterol has minimal effect. The 2015 Dietary Guidelines Advisory Committee quietly removed the 300mg/day limit.
Mainstream claim
Fasting causes muscle loss
Short-term fasting under 72 hours does not cause meaningful muscle catabolism in trained individuals eating adequate protein. Growth hormone increases significantly during fasting -- a muscle-sparing adaptation.
What to actually track
Biomarkers worth caring about
LDL-P or ApoB, triglycerides under 100, HDL above 60, fasting insulin under 5, HbA1c under 5.4%, testosterone total and free, hs-CRP.
