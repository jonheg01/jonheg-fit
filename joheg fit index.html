What the science actually says
E
Whole eggs — eat as many as you want
Choline, leucine, fat-soluble vitamins A/D/E/K2, complete amino acids. The yolk is where all the nutrition lives. 4–6 whole eggs/day supported by independent research. Dietary cholesterol limits dropped from USDA guidelines in 2015.
6–7g P/egg5g F/egg~80 cal
B
80/20 ground beef — your best muscle-building food
Creatine, carnitine, zinc, iron, B12, complete protein. Do NOT buy lean beef. Saturated fat from ruminants is associated with higher testosterone in men. Grass-fed improves omega-3 ratio. 8–12 oz/day is ideal.
22g P/4oz17g F/4oz~240 cal
M
Whole milk (non-homogenized) — excellent choice
Non-homogenized preserves fat globule structure. Contains CLA with evidence for body recomposition. Casein protein digests slowly — perfect before bed. Whole-milk heart disease link walked back in independent meta-analyses.
8g P/cup8g F/cup12g C/cup~150 cal
A
Add: butter and tallow — replace all seed oils now
Cook in butter or beef tallow only. Seed oils (canola, soybean, sunflower, corn) oxidize at cooking temperatures generating inflammatory byproducts. Butter and tallow are stable at high heat. Single most impactful food swap you can make.
R
White rice — better than you have been told
Low in anti-nutrients compared to whole grains. Digests cleanly, replenishes muscle glycogen efficiently post-workout. Japanese populations eating white rice have among the lowest obesity and heart disease rates on earth. Time it post-workout.
45g C/cup cooked4g P~200 cal
L
Add: beef liver — once per week
Most nutrient-dense food on earth. More bioavailable B12, folate, copper, iron, zinc, and vitamin A than any supplement. Blend 4 oz raw liver and mix into raw ground beef at 20/80 ratio before cooking — you will not taste it.

What mainstream American nutrition gets wrong
Mainstream claim
Saturated fat causes heart disease
The original Seven Countries Study cherry-picked data. Multiple large independent meta-analyses found no significant association. The relevant markers are LDL particle size, triglycerides, and HDL — all of which improve on your food plan.
Mainstream claim
Dietary cholesterol raises blood cholesterol
For ~75% of people, dietary cholesterol has minimal effect on serum cholesterol. The 2015 Dietary Guidelines Advisory Committee quietly removed the 300mg/day limit.
Mainstream claim
Fasting causes muscle loss
Short-term fasting under 72 hours does not cause meaningful muscle catabolism in trained individuals eating adequate protein. Growth hormone increases significantly during a fast — a muscle-sparing adaptation.
What to actually track
Biomarkers worth caring about
Ask your doctor for: LDL-P or ApoB, triglycerides (target under 100), HDL (target above 60), fasting insulin (target under 5 uIU/mL), HbA1c (target under 5.4%), testosterone total and free, hs-CRP. Your food plan will likely improve every one of these.
